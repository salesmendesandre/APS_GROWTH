# APS-GROWTH I: The Technological and Social Foundation

## General Objectives and Justification
During the **2024-2025** academic year, the APS-GROWTH project laid the foundations not only for the digitization of the inclusive garden but also for the creation of a learning community around it. The main objective of this first year was to promote social inclusion and sustainability through the development and implementation of a **multimodal application** to manage the inclusive garden of the Lorenzo Milani Center and the SAS.

The social need was to create an environment where technology served as a bridge for inclusion, reducing cognitive and physical barriers for volunteers and users with special needs.

## Methodology and Development Phases

The project was structured into well-defined phases ranging from initial research to evaluation and optimization:

1. **Inclusive Design:** University students worked side by side with garden users to understand their daily needs, designing interfaces based on pictograms and high-contrast options.
2. **Smart Identification:** The use of QR codes and proximity beacons (BLE - Bluetooth Low Energy) was implemented. By simply approaching a plant, the user receives information about it directly on their mobile device.
3. **Task Management:** The application allowed for the registration of basic tasks, such as watering or removing adventitious vegetation, informing the user about each plant's needs through simple alerts.

## Learning Objectives

For the students of the University of Salamanca (Degrees in Computer Engineering, Biology, and Anthropology), the objectives went far beyond mere software development:
- Apply theoretical knowledge to solve real-world problems.
- Foster empathy and understanding towards people with diverse needs.
- Promote teamwork and interdisciplinary collaboration.

## Schedule and Impact

Planning spanned the entire academic year, ensuring that each phase (from the initial design to the physical deployment of beacons in the garden) was completed on time.

By empowering volunteers to manage their own space, an initial version of a truly human-centered technological ecosystem was established, closely aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 4, 10, and 11).
