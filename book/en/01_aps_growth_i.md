# APS-GROWTH I: The Technological and Social Foundation

## Overview
During the **2024-2025** academic year, the APS-GROWTH project laid the groundwork not only for the digitization of the inclusive garden but also for the creation of a learning community around it. The main goal of this first phase was to create an environment where technology served as a bridge for inclusion, developing a **multimodal web application** that reduced cognitive and physical barriers for the volunteers and users of the Lorenzo Milani Vocational Training Center.

## Social and Educational Dimension
Beyond the devices, the project initiated a dynamic of collaborative work. University students worked side by side with garden users to understand their daily needs, designing interfaces based on pictograms and high-contrast options. This ensured that the tool was truly useful and not an added barrier.

## Developed Technology
- **Smart Identification:** QR codes and proximity beacons (BLE - Bluetooth Low Energy) were implemented. Simply by approaching a plant, the user receives information about it.
- **Task Management:** The application enabled the registration of basic tasks, such as watering or weeding, informing the user about the needs of each plant through adapted interfaces and simple alerts.

## Impact
This initial stage allowed connecting technology with the rural and social environment, promoting **inclusion and autonomous participation**. By empowering volunteers with functional diversity to manage their own space, the first version of a truly human-centric technological ecosystem was established.
