# APS-GROWTH II: IoT Integration and Botanical Analysis

## Overview
In the **2025-2026** academic year, the project took a qualitative leap, moving from manual task management to active environmental data collection. The second phase focused on the **sensorization of the garden**, uniting the capabilities of Computer Engineering with the scientific knowledge of the Biology and Anthropology departments.

## Scientific and Pedagogical Dimension
Simply collecting data was not enough; it was necessary to interpret it. The Botany team created technical data sheets and defined which parameters were critical for the different species of cultivated plants. Thanks to this, students and garden users were able to learn about plant physiology in real-time, observing how temperature or light affected crop growth, turning the garden into a living laboratory.

## Developed Technology
- **Sensor Network (IoT):** Sensors were installed to measure soil moisture, temperature, and light intensity.
- **LoRaWAN Communication:** Data transmission was carried out through long-range, low-power networks, ideal for open and agricultural spaces without the need for WiFi.
- **Smart Alerts:** Notification systems based on real-time data were developed. These alerts were integrated into the application with improved visualization to ensure cognitive accessibility for users.

## Impact
The project evolved from manual registration to **active real-time monitoring**, providing users with recommendations based on empirical data and consolidating a culture of ecological care supported by scientific evidence.
