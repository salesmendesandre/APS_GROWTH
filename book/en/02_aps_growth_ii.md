# APS-GROWTH II: IoT Integration and Botanical Analysis

## Technological and Scientific Evolution
In the **2025-2026** academic year, the project made a qualitative leap from simple task management to the active collection of environmental data. The second year focused on the **sensorization of the garden**, combining the technological capabilities of the Computer Science Area with the scientific rigor of the Biology and Anthropology departments.

The overall objective was to reinforce social inclusion and sustainability through an application connected to environmental sensors via the low-power LoRaWAN network, allowing field information to reach users directly without relying on complex WiFi infrastructures.

## Sensor Network Implementation

The architectural and network system was based on the following key elements:

1. **Sensor Nodes:** Nodes equipped with sensors were installed to measure critical variables such as soil moisture (capacitive sensors), ambient temperature and humidity (BME280), and light intensity (BH1750).
2. **LoRaWAN Communication:** Data transmission was guaranteed through LoRa modules connected to an outdoor Gateway, ideal for agricultural environments.
3. **Energy Autonomy:** The nodes were equipped with solar panels and Li-ion batteries, ensuring an ecological and continuous deployment within waterproof enclosures (IP65).

## Botanical and Pedagogical Dimension

Simply collecting data was not enough; it was necessary to interpret it. The Botany team developed **technical data sheets** and defined which parameters were critical for the different plant species grown.

Thanks to this, students and garden users could learn about plant physiology in real-time, observing how environmental variations affected crop growth and turning the garden into a true living laboratory.

## Community Impact

The project evolved into **active real-time monitoring**, providing recommendations based on empirical data. Smart alert systems were developed and integrated into the application with an improved display, guaranteeing cognitive accessibility at all times and consolidating a culture of ecological care.
