# APS-GROWTH III: Automation and Autonomy

## Autonomous Management and Smart Irrigation
For the **2026-2027** academic year, the goal is to close the technological and ecological loop, moving from mere observation to **automated action**. The third phase focuses on the evolution of the system towards a **smart irrigation system** capable of making autonomous decisions transparently, always maintaining the focus on interdisciplinary education and user inclusion.

## Integration of Actuators and Algorithms

The foundation of this phase lies in transforming data into concrete physical actions. To this end, the project addresses:

1. **Smart Actuators:** Integration of 12V solenoid valves, flow meters, and control relays to physically manage the flow of water in different areas of the garden, supported by LoRa microcontrollers.
2. **Predictive Algorithms:** Rigorous water stress thresholds were established with the Botany team. The system cross-references these data with sensor measurements (soil moisture, temperature) to activate irrigation only when the plants truly need it.
3. **Physical Infrastructure:** Deployment of all necessary hydraulic infrastructure (pipes, branches, manual shut-off valves) and protection systems and final electrical installation to operate safely outdoors.

## Empowerment, Transparency, and Explainability

The biggest challenge of this phase is not just watering automatically, but preventing the technology from becoming a "black box." The primary goal is for users to understand *why* the system decides to water.

To achieve this, Computer Engineering students have redesigned the application to explain, through highly accessible interfaces, the decisions made by the machine. This reinforces learning about efficient water use and plant water stress.

## Long-Term Impact

This phase achieves true **sustainable water management** and complete technical autonomy. The system empowers users, who transition from being manual operators to supervisors of a transparent automated assistant, keeping them deeply involved in a continuous process of ecological learning and technological inclusion.
