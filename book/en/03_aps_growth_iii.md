# APS-GROWTH III: Automation and Autonomy

## Overview
For the **2026-2027** academic year, the goal is to close the technological and ecological loop, moving from observation to **automated action**. The third phase focuses on designing an **intelligent irrigation system** capable of making autonomous decisions, but always maintaining an approach centered on education and user inclusion.

## Empowerment and Transparency
The biggest challenge of this phase is not just watering automatically, but preventing technology from becoming a "black box." The goal is for users to understand *why* the system decides to water. To this end, the students redesigned the application to explain, through highly accessible interfaces, the decisions made by the machine, thus reinforcing learning about efficient water use and plant water stress.

## Developed Technology
- **Smart Actuators:** Integration of electrovalves and flowmeters to physically manage water flow in different areas of the garden.
- **Predictive Algorithms:** Rigorous water stress thresholds were established with the Botany team to activate irrigation only when plants really need it, optimizing every drop of water.
- **Architectural Redesign:** The application was internally renewed to support the processing of historical data, maintaining an intuitive interface that makes the decision process transparent to the user.

## Impact
This phase achieves true **sustainable water management** and complete technical autonomy. The system empowers users, who supervise an automated assistant, keeping them involved in a continuous process of ecological learning and technological inclusion.
