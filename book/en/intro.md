# Introduction

Welcome to **APS-GROWTH**, a Service-Learning initiative combining technological innovation, social inclusion, and environmental sustainability.

## What is the APS-GROWTH project?

**APS-GROWTH** (Multimodal Application for the Inclusive Management and Learning of an Educational and Ecological Garden) is a project jointly developed by students and professors from the faculties of **Computer Science and Biology** at the University of Salamanca (USAL), in close collaboration with the **Lorenzo Milani Vocational Training Center**.

The project was born with the aim of facilitating the management and maintenance of an inclusive garden through accessible technology, ensuring that anyone, regardless of their physical or cognitive capabilities, can actively participate and learn about horticulture and sustainability in an increasingly autonomous way.

To achieve this, an unprecedented interdisciplinary effort has been established: while Computer Science students design the software infrastructure, hardware, and IoT interfaces, Biology and Anthropology students provide the scientific rigor by defining water stress thresholds and the vital needs of each plant species.

## Project Evolution

Throughout its three annual phases, the project has grown in ambition and technological complexity to create a complete intelligent ecosystem:

- **APS-GROWTH I (Academic Year 2024-2025)**: Creation of the technological foundation. A multimodal and accessible web application was developed that allows identifying plants using QR codes and proximity beacons (BLE). The tool facilitated the notification of basic tasks (watering, weeding) and the registration of actions, laying the groundwork for inclusive participation.
- **APS-GROWTH II (Academic Year 2025-2026)**: Integration of the Internet of Things (IoT). Low-power environmental sensors connected via LoRa/LoRaWAN technology were incorporated to measure soil moisture, temperature, and light. This allowed a shift from manual observation to obtaining real-time data, generating smart alerts, and offering data visualization adapted to the garden's users.
- **APS-GROWTH III (Academic Year 2026-2027)**: Automation and active intelligence. The project evolved towards an autonomous irrigation management system. By integrating physical actuators (electrovalves and flowmeters) with historical environmental data and predictive algorithms, the system can make automatic watering decisions based on the actual needs of the plants. The entire decision-making process is made transparent to users through a redesigned, modular, and intuitive application, closing the loop between technology, pedagogy, and ecology.

## Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

This project contributes directly to the 2030 Agenda, promoting:
- **SDG 4 (Quality Education)**: Active and interdisciplinary learning in a real-world environment.
- **SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure)**: Practical integration of IoT technologies, LoRa, and automated systems in educational contexts.
- **SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities)**: Adapted and accessible technology that eliminates participation barriers.
- **SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities)**: Improvement in the management of community green spaces.
- **SDG 15 (Life on Land)**: Responsible water use, species preservation, and scientific care of the plant environment.
